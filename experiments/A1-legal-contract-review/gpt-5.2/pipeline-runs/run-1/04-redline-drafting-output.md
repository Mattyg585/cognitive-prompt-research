### I-001 — Provider use of Feedback and Usage Data  (YELLOW; Should-have)
**Clause**: 1.4 (Feedback and Usage Data)

**Current language**:
> **1.4 Feedback and Usage Data.** Customer may, but is not required to, give Provider Feedback, in which case Customer gives Feedback "AS IS". Provider may use all Feedback freely without any restriction or obligation. In addition, Provider may collect and analyze Usage Data, and Provider may freely use Usage Data to maintain, improve, enhance, and promote Provider's products and services without restriction or obligation. However, Provider may only disclose Usage Data to others if the Usage Data is aggregated and does not identify Customer or Users.

**Proposed redline**:
Replace Section 1.4 with:

[DELETE: “Provider may use all Feedback freely without any restriction or obligation.”]

[ADD: “Provider may use Feedback for any lawful purpose without restriction; provided that Provider will not publicly attribute Feedback to Customer without Customer’s prior written consent.”]

[DELETE: “Provider may freely use Usage Data to maintain, improve, enhance, and promote Provider's products and services without restriction or obligation.”]

[ADD: “Provider may collect and analyze Usage Data, and may use Usage Data solely (i) to provide, maintain, support, secure, and improve the Product and Provider’s products and services, and (ii) for internal business analytics. Provider will not use Usage Data to market or promote Provider’s products or services in a manner that identifies Customer or any User, or that publicly references Customer, without Customer’s prior written consent.”]

[ADD: “Provider may disclose Usage Data to third parties only if the Usage Data is aggregated and does not identify Customer or Users, and only for purposes consistent with this Section.”]

**Fallback positions**:
- Keep “promote” but add: “provided that any promotion will not identify Customer or any User, and will not reference Customer by name or logo, without prior written consent.”
- Add an opt-out: Customer may opt out of non-essential Usage Data uses upon written notice.

**External rationale (shareable)**: Customer is comfortable with reasonable telemetry/analytics and service improvement, but needs to prevent identifiable marketing use and ensure disclosures are only aggregated and non-identifying.

**Internal notes (optional)**: Confirm internal policy on analytics/telemetry and whether any “Usage Data” could include sensitive metadata.
