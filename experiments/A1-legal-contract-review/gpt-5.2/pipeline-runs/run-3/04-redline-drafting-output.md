### I-001 — AI/ML training and enhancement using Usage Data and Customer Content  (RED; Must-have)
**Clause**: Section 1.6 (Machine Learning)
**Current language**: 
> **1.6 Machine Learning.** Usage Data and Customer Content may be used to develop, train, or enhance artificial intelligence or machine learning models that are part of Provider's products and services, including third-party components of the Product, and Customer authorizes Provider to process its Usage Data and Customer Content for such purposes. However, (a) Usage Data and Customer Content must be aggregated before it can be used for these purposes, and (b) Provider will use commercially reasonable efforts consistent with industry standard technology to de-identify Usage Data and Customer Content before such use. Due to the nature of artificial intelligence and machine learning, information generated by these features may be incorrect or inaccurate. Product features that include artificial intelligence or machine learning models are not human and are not a substitute for human oversight.

**Proposed redline**:
Replace Section 1.6 with:

> **1.6 Machine Learning.** {+Provider may use Usage Data to develop, train, or enhance artificial intelligence or machine learning models that are part of Provider's products and services solely to provide, maintain, and improve the Product for Customer.+} {+Provider will not use Customer Content to develop, train, or enhance any artificial intelligence or machine learning models (including any third‑party models or components) except to the extent Customer expressly opts in in writing (which may be on an Order Form) for a specifically described use case.+} 
>
> {+If Customer opts in, Provider will: (a) use only aggregated data for such purposes; (b) de-identify data prior to such use; (c) not disclose Customer Content to any third party for model training without Customer’s prior written consent; and (d) apply the Privacy & Security and Confidentiality obligations to any retained training datasets and derived artifacts. Provider will document the opt-in scope, retention periods, and any subprocessors/third-party components involved.+}
>
> Nothing in this section will reduce or limit Provider's obligations regarding Personal Data that may be contained in Usage Data or Customer Content under Applicable Data Protection Laws.

**Fallback positions**:
- Allow AI/ML training on **Usage Data only**, expressly excluding Customer Content, and expressly excluding third‑party components.
- If Provider insists on Customer Content use: make it **opt-in per Customer/Order Form**, limited to the specific Product feature(s), with a right to revoke prospectively and a defined retention period.

**External rationale (shareable)**: Customer needs to ensure its content is used only to deliver the contracted service, not to train models (especially where third‑party components may be involved). Opt-in and scoped controls are commercially common for sensitive data governance and IP protection.
**Internal notes (optional)**: Decision point: whether any AI feature requires Customer Content for functionality; if yes, insist on an explicit opt-in addendum with retention/subprocessor transparency.
