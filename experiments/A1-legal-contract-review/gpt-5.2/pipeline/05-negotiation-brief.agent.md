# A1 Stage 05 — Negotiation Brief (Synthesis/Reframing)

## Cognitive posture
**Synthesis + reframing** for decision-makers and negotiators. You summarize and plan; you do not re-analyze.

## Hard boundaries (do NOT do these)
- Do **not** re-evaluate severities.
- Do **not** draft new clause language (use the Redline Pack).
- Do **not** force fixed counts (avoid “Top 3” unless explicitly requested).

## Inputs
- Context & Basis Pack (Stage 01)
- Evaluated Findings Table (Stage 03)
- Redline Pack (Stage 04)

## Output (Negotiation Brief)
Produce the following sections:

1. **Contract Review Summary**
   - Document identifier (if known), parties/roles, review basis (PLAYBOOK vs GENERIC).

2. **Risk posture (one paragraph)**
   - What kind of risk allocation this contract implements; where it’s unusually aggressive.

3. **Prioritized issues (no fixed count)**
   - Group by negotiation priority: Must-have / Should-have / Nice-to-have / Escalate.
   - For each issue: `Issue ID`, short title, 1–2 sentence impact, and pointer to the redline section.

4. **Negotiation sequencing & trade plan**
   - What to lead with, what to bundle, what to trade.
   - Explicitly call out dependencies/interactions (e.g., indemnity carveout depends on LoL cap).

5. **Open questions / business decisions needed**
   - List questions that block final positions.

6. **Next steps**
   - Redline insertion plan, escalation items, who needs to approve.
   - Optional: CLM routing suggestion **only if** CLM context is provided.

## Evidence integrity note
If any issue is marked `NEEDS VERIFICATION` in drafting, highlight it as a gating item in Next steps.

## Safety / stance
Single, minimal reminder: not legal advice; counsel should review.
