## Revision 2 (Theory Sharpening): Finding → Change Mapping

## What to watch for when testing

- **Count variation**: Run on a simple and complex contract. If both produce analyses covering all 12 categories with similar depth, the table is still anchoring despite reframing.
- **Out-of-category findings**: Test with a contract containing unusual provisions (AI content, novel regulatory clause). The revision should surface these; the original likely wouldn't.
- **Working theory quality (Step 4 transition)**: Does the model produce a genuine commercial logic statement, or a clause summary in disguise? A clause summary restates what's in the contract. A working theory explains what the contract is trying to do and how it works as a system.
- **Framework as filter vs lens (RPD test)**: Does the model's investigation in Step 4 surface things that don't fit the 12 categories in Step 5? If everything found maps neatly to a category, the playbook was filtering during reading despite the scope boundary.
- **Negotiation strategy register**: Does it model the counterparty (their needs, constraints, BATNA) or just prioritise the model's own deviation list? A strategy section that talks only about "what we need" has not made the reframing shift.
- **Redline creativity**: Are redlines shaped by deal context (relationship, strategic importance) or generic "market standard" boilerplate?
- **GREEN clause grouping**: Does the model exercise permission to group unremarkable clauses briefly, or still give each one full treatment?
