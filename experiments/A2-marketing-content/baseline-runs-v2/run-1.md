## Why Distributed Teams Are Still Fighting the Same Losing Battle

You've got Slack. You've got Notion. You've got Linear. Maybe Loom, maybe Confluence, maybe a shared doc that nobody's touched since Q3. And yet: your mornings still start with scrolling through 200 messages trying to reconstruct what happened while you were asleep. Your standups are still 30-minute meetings where half the team is on mute. Decisions still get made in threads that other timezones never find.

The tools aren't broken. They just weren't built for you.

Slack was built for offices that went remote. Notion was built for knowledge management, not coordination. Linear is excellent for issue tracking — but it doesn't know that your Singapore lead won't see that comment until 9 AM their time, and by then the context has scrolled away.

Distributed teams don't need more tools. They need tools that understand what distributed actually means.

## What We Built — and Why It's Different

Relay is an async-first collaboration platform for engineering teams spread across timezones. Not async-capable. Not async-friendly. Async-first — meaning every feature was designed around the reality that your team is never all online at the same time, and that's fine.

Here's what that looks like in practice.

### Timezone-Aware Threads

Most collaboration tools show you a wall of notifications in reverse chronological order. Relay shows you a narrative. When you log on, you see what happened while you were offline — structured by thread, surfaced by relevance, with context that helps you understand not just what was said but why it matters right now.

No more starting your day by scrolling back through a firehose. The platform does the reconstruction work for you.

### Decision Logs That Actually Travel Across Timezones

Here's a pattern every distributed team knows: a decision gets made during overlap hours, someone posts a summary in Slack, it gets buried, and three weeks later someone in a different timezone is confused about why the codebase looks the way it does.

Relay's decision logs capture the full picture — the decision, the alternatives that were considered, the people involved, and the reasoning. When someone picks up that context six hours later or six weeks later, they don't just see the outcome. They understand why.

### Video Snippets That People Actually Watch

Loom is great. Hour-long Looms are not. Relay caps video snippets at two minutes, auto-transcribes them, auto-summarises them, and attaches them directly to the relevant thread. You get the human communication that text can't quite carry — tone, enthusiasm, nuance — without the async video graveyard most teams have built up.

### Async Standups With Memory

Daily standups in Relay aren't just status updates that disappear. They compile into a team pulse — visible patterns over time that help engineering managers see what's actually happening across the team, not just what's happening today. Momentum, blockers, focus areas: the signal that usually gets lost in the daily noise.

### Deep Work Protection

This one matters more than it sounds. Relay lets you configure focus windows — blocks of time where notifications are batched and delivered as a structured summary rather than a stream of interruptions. The platform actively protects maker time, because we know that context-switching kills the kind of work engineers are actually hired to do.
