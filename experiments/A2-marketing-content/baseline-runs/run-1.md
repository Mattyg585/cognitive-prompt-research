## The Problem: Collaboration Tools That Pretend Everyone Is Online at the Same Time

Most collaboration tools bolt async features onto a fundamentally synchronous design. Slack has threads, but they still assume real-time conversation. Notion is great for documentation, but it does not help you understand what happened while you were offline. Loom lets you record video, but a 45-minute recording is not an async-friendly artifact.

The deeper issue is that these tools treat timezone differences as an inconvenience to work around rather than a core design constraint. Distributed teams end up layering tool after tool — Slack for chat, Notion for docs, Linear for tickets, Loom for walkthroughs, Google Docs for meeting notes — and then spending half their energy keeping context synchronized across all of them.

The cost is real. Teams hold more meetings to compensate for lost context. Decisions get made by whoever happens to be online. Engineers lose deep work time to notification noise. And the people furthest from headquarters feel the most left out.

## What Relay Does Differently: Async-First by Design

Relay is not another tool asking you to bolt async habits onto a synchronous workflow. It is a collaborative workspace built around the premise that your team will never all be online at the same time — and that this is fine.

### Timezone-Aware Threads

This is the feature beta users mentioned most — 80% of feedback highlighted it. When you open a thread, Relay automatically structures what happened while you were away into a readable narrative. Not a wall of messages you have to scroll through. Not a notification count. A structured summary that gives you context, decisions, and open questions — so you can contribute meaningfully without asking everyone to repeat themselves.

As one beta user, a VP of Engineering at a 120-person company, put it: "We stopped having catch-up meetings. The catch-up just... happens."

### Decision Logs That Capture the Why

Every decision in Relay captures more than just the outcome. It records the context that led to the decision, the alternatives that were considered, and who was involved. When a teammate in another timezone sees that a direction was chosen, they do not just see the result — they understand the reasoning. This eliminates the "why did we decide this?" messages that clog up async channels and trigger unnecessary re-litigation of settled questions.

### Video Snippets, Not Video Marathons

Relay supports two-minute video updates that auto-transcribe, auto-summarize, and attach directly to the relevant thread. No more hour-long Loom recordings that nobody watches. The constraint is deliberate: short, focused updates that respect the viewer's time. The transcript and summary mean you can get the content without watching the video at all, which matters when timezone collaboration means you are catching up on a dozen updates at once.

### Async Standups That Reveal Patterns

Daily standups in Relay are structured updates that compile into a team pulse view. Instead of a status snapshot that is stale by tomorrow, you get visible patterns across weeks — where the team is spending time, where blockers persist, and where momentum is building. Engineering managers get signal without scheduling a meeting.

### Deep Work Protection

Relay actively protects maker time. Configurable focus windows batch and summarize notifications so engineers are not interrupted every time someone posts an update. The platform treats uninterrupted work as a feature, not a side effect of muting channels.
