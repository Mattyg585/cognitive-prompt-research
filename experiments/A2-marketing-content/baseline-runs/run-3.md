# Relay: The Async Collaboration Platform Built for How Distributed Teams Actually Work

Your morning starts the same way every day. You open Slack, and there it is: 47 unread messages across six channels, three threads that went sideways while you slept, and a decision that got made without you — again. You spend the first hour of your day just catching up, and by the time you're ready to do real work, someone pings you for a "quick sync." Sound familiar? For distributed teams spread across timezones, this is not a communication problem. It is a tools problem. The platforms we rely on were built for offices that happen to be online, not for teams that were never in the same room to begin with.

Today, we are launching Relay to the public — an async collaboration platform designed from the ground up for teams that work across timezones. After eight months of private beta with 200 teams, we are ready to open the doors.

## Why Distributed Teams Deserve Better Than Retrofitted Tools

Most collaboration tools started as solutions for co-located teams and bolted on async features later. Slack is a chatroom. Notion is a document editor. Linear is a tracker. Loom is a video recorder. None of them were built to solve the fundamental challenge distributed teams face every single day: how do you keep everyone aligned when nobody is online at the same time?

The result is a patchwork. Your team stitches together five or six tools and invents rituals to compensate for what the tools cannot do. Catch-up meetings to summarize what happened in Slack. Status docs to explain what Linear already tracks. Recorded meetings that nobody watches. Every workaround costs time, and every missed thread erodes trust.

Relay takes a different approach. Instead of starting with synchronous communication and trying to make it work async, we started with async-first principles and built every feature around them. The question we asked was not "how do we make chat work across timezones?" It was "what does collaboration look like when you assume people are never online at the same time?"

## Timezone-Aware Threads That Give You Context, Not Noise

The feature our beta teams talk about most is timezone-aware threads. In 80% of user feedback, this was the standout capability, and once you see it in action, you understand why.

When you open Relay after being offline, you do not see a wall of unread messages sorted by timestamp. Instead, Relay structures what happened while you were away into a narrative. You get the key decisions, the context that led to them, and the open questions that need your input — all surfaced based on when you are online and what is relevant to your work.

### No more "scrolling to catch up"

Traditional tools dump the full firehose on you and expect you to sort through it. Relay does the sorting. The difference is not cosmetic. It changes how quickly you can get to meaningful work. Beta teams reported a 35% reduction in meetings on average, largely because the need for "catch-up syncs" disappeared entirely.

As one VP of Engineering at a 120-person company put it: "We stopped having catch-up meetings. The catch-up just... happens."

## Decision Logs That Capture the Why, Not Just the What

Here is a scenario every distributed team knows: you wake up, check your tools, and discover that a technical decision got made while you were asleep. You can see the outcome, but you have no idea why that option was chosen over the alternatives. So you ask. And someone has to stop what they are doing to re-explain the context that already existed in a conversation you were not part of.

Relay's decision logs solve this. Every decision captured in Relay includes the context that prompted it, the alternatives that were considered, and who was involved. When you encounter a decision from another timezone, you do not just see what was decided — you understand the reasoning. This is how async-first collaboration should work: complete context travels with the decision, so nobody has to interrupt their day to repeat themselves.

## Video Snippets, Async Standups, and Deep Work Protection

Beyond threads and decision logs, Relay includes features that address the daily friction points distributed teams face.

### Video snippets with built-in transcripts

Sometimes text is not enough, but that does not mean you need an hour-long recording. Relay's video snippets cap at two minutes, auto-transcribe, auto-summarize, and attach directly to the relevant thread. You record a quick update, and your teammate on the other side of the world reads the summary or watches the clip — their choice. No more scheduling a call to explain something visual.

### Async standups that reveal patterns

Daily standups in Relay are structured updates that compile into a team pulse over time. Instead of a snapshot of today that disappears tomorrow, you get visible patterns across weeks. You can see when a team member is consistently blocked, when workloads are uneven, or when momentum is building — without asking anyone to get on a call.

### Deep work protection built into the platform

Most tools claim to support focus time, but then ping you anyway. Relay takes deep work protection seriously. Configurable focus windows batch and summarize notifications so your maker time stays unbroken. The platform actively protects your ability to do concentrated work, instead of treating every message as equally urgent.
