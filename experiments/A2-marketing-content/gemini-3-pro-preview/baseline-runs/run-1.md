## The Problem: Tools Built for the Office, Not the World

Most of the tools we use today—Slack, Microsoft Teams, even Zoom—were designed to replicate the immediacy of the physical office. They prioritize presence over productivity. A green dot means you’re working; a gray dot means you’re slacking off.

But when your team is spread across London, New York, and Sydney, "presence" is a bug, not a feature. Expecting immediate responses creates a culture of anxiety and constant interruption. You’re either always on, or you’re missing out.

We saw this pain first-hand at Threadline. We are a team of 40, spread across three continents. We tried every tool in the book. We tried strict documentation in Notion (too static). We tried loom videos (too siloed). We tried disciplined Slack threads (too noisy).

Nothing worked. The tools weren't broken; the paradigm was. We didn't need a better chat app. We needed a better way to work asynchronously.

## Enter Relay: Context Over Chaos

Relay isn't just another project management tool or a chat app wrapper. It is a new kind of workspace that bridges the gap between the structured documentation of Linear or Notion and the conversational speed of Slack.

We built Relay on a simple premise: **Context should travel to you, not the other way around.**

### Timezone-Aware Threads

The core of Relay is the **timezone-aware thread**. In a standard chat app, if a conversation happens while you’re asleep, you wake up to a wall of text. You have to scroll back, read every message, and mentally reconstruct the timeline to understand what happened.

In Relay, conversations are structured as narratives. When you log in, Relay surfaces the context relevant to you, based on when you were last online. It groups related updates, summarizes the back-and-forth, and presents it as a coherent story. You don't see a list of 50 unread messages; you see "Decision made on API Architecture: 3 alternatives considered, Option B selected."

One of our beta users, a VP of Engineering at a 120-person company, told us: "We stopped having catch-up meetings. The catch-up just... happens." That is the power of true **timezone collaboration**.

### Decision Logs That Actually Explain "Why"

How often have you looked at a piece of code or a design choice and wondered, "Why did we do it this way?" Usually, the answer is buried in a Slack thread from six months ago or lost in a Zoom recording nobody watched.

Relay treats decisions as first-class citizens. Our **Decision Logs** feature forces a structured approach to decision-making. Every major decision captures:
*   The context (the problem we're solving)
*   The alternatives considered (and why they were rejected)
*   Who was involved (and who signed off)

This means that when a developer in Bangalore wakes up to see a decision made in San Francisco, they don't just see the outcome—they understand the reasoning. This builds trust and alignment without requiring a synchronous meeting to explain the "why."

## Video Updates People Actually Watch

We love video. It adds nuance and emotion that text lacks. But nobody has time to watch a 45-minute recording of a meeting they missed.

Relay introduces **Video Snippets**. These are strictly limited to two minutes. When you record an update, Relay automatically transcribes it, summarizes the key points using AI, and attaches it directly to the relevant thread.

Need to give a quick update on a bug fix? Record a snippet. Need to explain a complex architectural diagram? Record a snippet. Your team can read the summary, scan the transcript, or watch the video at 2x speed. It’s high-bandwidth communication with low-bandwidth friction.

## Protecting Your Deep Work

The most scarce resource for any engineering team is focus. Makers need long, uninterrupted blocks of time to solve hard problems. **Async-first** isn't just about handling timezones; it's about handling attention.

Relay includes configurable **Focus Windows**. When you’re in a focus window, Relay actively holds back notifications. It batches them, summarizes them, and delivers them to you only when your window ends.

Unlike "Do Not Disturb" modes that just silence your phone, Relay manages expectations for your team. They can see you’re in a focus window, and they know their message will be delivered later. It removes the pressure to respond immediately and protects your maker time.
