## The "Real-Time" Trap

We’ve all been there. You’re in London, your PM is in New York, and your lead engineer is in San Francisco. To get anything done, someone has to sacrifice their evening or their morning. You schedule a "quick sync" that turns into an hour-long debate, mostly re-hashing context for the people who just woke up.

The modern tool stack is obsessed with presence. Green dots. Typing indicators. "Read" receipts. These features are great for chatting, but terrible for deep work. They create a culture of immediacy where the fastest response wins, not the best one.

Real-time communication has a hidden tax:
*   **Context switching:** It takes an average of 23 minutes to get back into focus after an interruption.
*   **Timezone inequality:** The "headquarters" timezone dominates decision-making simply because they are awake when the conversation happens.
*   **Meeting bloat:** When text fails, we default to meetings. Our beta teams reported that before Relay, they spent up to 40% of their week in status updates.

Relay is different. We built it to solve the specific coordination costs of **distributed teams**. We believe that async collaboration isn't just about writing things down; it’s about structuring communication so that timezones don't matter.

## Introducing Relay: Async-First by Design

Relay isn’t just another project management tool. It’s a dedicated space for the conversations that actually move work forward. Think of it as the missing link between your ticket tracker (Linear/Jira) and your chat app (Slack).

Where chat is fleeting and docs are static, Relay is narrative. It’s built around **timezone-aware threads**. When you open a thread in Relay, you don't just see a wall of text. You see a structured conversation that surfaces the most relevant context based on when *you* are logging in.

If a decision was made while you were asleep, Relay highlights it. It groups the back-and-forth into a coherent summary, so you can catch up in minutes, not hours. It’s like having a personal secretary who sat in on every meeting and took perfect notes, just for you.

## Features That Protect Your Focus

We didn't just want to reduce meetings; we wanted to protect the time you get back. Relay is opinionated about how work should happen.

### 1. Decision Logs That Actually Explain "Why"
How often do you revisit a decision six months later and wonder, "What were we thinking?" Most tools only capture the outcome. Relay captures the journey.

Our **Decision Logs** feature forces structured debate. You outline the context, the alternatives considered, and the key stakeholders involved. When a decision is finalized, it’s locked and indexed. People in other timezones don't just see the result; they understand the reasoning. This builds trust and alignment without requiring everyone to be in the same room.

### 2. Video Snippets, Not Monologues
Sometimes text isn't enough. But nobody watches a 45-minute Loom recording of a code walkthrough. Relay introduces **Video Snippets**—capped at 2 minutes.

These aren't just raw video files. Relay automatically transcribes them, summarizes the key points, and attaches them directly to the relevant thread. You can watch the video, read the transcript, or just scan the summary. It’s the highest-fidelity communication with the lowest possible friction.

### 3. Deep Work Protection
The most valuable asset for an engineering team is uninterrupted focus time. Relay respects that. Our **Focus Windows** feature lets you batch notifications. Instead of a constant drip-feed of interruptions, you get a digest of what happened during your focus block.

You can configure Relay to only notify you of urgent items (like a blocker on a launch) while batching the rest for your morning coffee or end-of-day review. The platform actively protects your maker time.
