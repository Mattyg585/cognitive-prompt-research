# Your Team Isn’t Distributed. It’s Just Fragmented.

The promise of remote work was freedom. The reality for most engineering teams is a never-ending game of catch-up.

You wake up to a wall of Slack notifications. You spend your first two hours reading through threads that happened while you slept, trying to piece together context from fragmented comments. Then, you jump into a "sync" meeting that could have been an email, just to make sure everyone is on the same page. By the time you actually start coding or designing, your "focus window" is already closing.

We built **Relay**, the first true **async collaboration platform** for distributed teams, because we were tired of living in that loop. Today, after eight months of private beta and 200 teams helping us refine the vision, we are opening Relay to the public.

## The "Always-On" Trap

Most collaboration tools—Slack, Microsoft Teams, even Zoom—were designed to simulate the office. They prioritize presence. The green dot is the new "butt in seat."

But when your team spans London, New York, and San Francisco, presence is a bug, not a feature. simulating a synchronous office across timezones results in one of two things:
1.  **Burnout**: People stay online late to catch the "handover."
2.  **Chaos**: Decisions happen in a vacuum, and the people who weren't online just have to deal with the fallout.

We call this "fragmentation." Your team is technically working together, but effectively, you're working alone, constantly interrupted by the ghosts of conversations past.

## Enter Relay: Built for the Async-First Reality

Relay isn't just another place to chat. It's an **async collaboration platform** designed from the ground up to handle the reality of timezones. We don't want you to be "always on." We want you to be "effectively off."

Here is how we do it.

### Timezone-Aware Threads
This is the feature our beta users loved most (80% of them cited it as a game-changer).

In Relay, conversations aren't just a linear feed. They are structured narratives. When you log in, Relay surfaces the context you need based on *when* you are reading. It groups updates, summarizes the back-and-forth that happened while you were asleep, and presents it as a coherent story. You don't "catch up" on chat logs; you read the digest.

### Decision Logs That Actually Explain "Why"
How many times have you woken up to find a decision was made, but you have no idea why?

Relay includes dedicated Decision Logs. Every major decision captures the context, the alternatives considered, and who was involved. It's not just a record of the outcome; it's a record of the *thinking*. This means your team in Tokyo understands the reasoning of the team in Berlin without needing a 7 AM video call to explain it.

### Video Snippets (Because Nobody Watches Hour-Long Recordings)
We know sometimes text isn't enough. But let's be honest: nobody watches that 45-minute Loom recording of a code review.

Relay enforces a 2-minute limit on video updates. But we didn't stop there. Every video is **auto-transcribed and auto-summarized**. These snippets attach directly to the relevant thread. You can watch if you want, or you can scan the summary in 10 seconds. It’s high-fidelity communication with low-bandwidth consumption.

### Async Standups & Deep Work Protection
The daily standup is the enemy of deep work. Relay replaces it with structured async updates that compile into a team pulse. You can see patterns over weeks, not just today's status.

More importantly, Relay protects your maker time. You can configure "focus windows" where notifications are batched and summarized. We don't just let you turn off notifications; we actively help you manage your attention.
