# Finally, an Async Collaboration Platform That Kills the Catch-Up Meeting

You know the drill. It’s 10 PM in London, 2 PM in San Francisco, and 6 AM in Sydney. You’re all on a call because it’s the only time everyone is awake. Nobody is at their best. Half the team is tired; the other half is barely caffeinated.

You aren't meeting to do work. You're meeting to *talk about* work. You're meeting to catch up on what happened while you were asleep.

We call this the Timezone Tax. And for the last eight months, we’ve been building the solution to repeal it.

Today, we’re publicly launching **Relay**, the **async collaboration platform** built specifically for distributed engineering teams.

## The Problem Isn't Timezones, It's Context

Most teams try to solve the timezone problem with tools built for the office. Slack is great for real-time chatter, but it’s a firehose of noise when you wake up to 400 unread messages. Notion is excellent for static docs, but it’s where context goes to die.

So you schedule a meeting. You trade deep work for synchronization.

We built Relay on a different premise: **Context should travel without you.**

If your tools actually worked, you wouldn't need a meeting to explain why a decision was made. You wouldn't need a "quick sync" to get the status update. The platform would handle the narrative, leaving you free to do the engineering.

## Meet Relay: Built Async-First from the Ground Up

Relay isn't just another project management tool. It combines the narrative power of a document with the velocity of a chat, structured specifically for teams that never sit in the same room.

### Timezone-Aware Threads
In Relay, conversations aren't just a linear list of messages. Our **timezone-aware threads** automatically surface relevant context based on *your* working hours.

When you log on, you don't see a wall of notifications. You see a structured narrative of what happened while you slept. The platform connects the dots for you, turning chaotic chatter into a coherent story. This is the feature our beta users mentioned in 80% of their feedback—it’s the difference between feeling buried and feeling informed.

### Decisions Need a Paper Trail, Not a Chat Log
How often do you ask, "Why did we decide this?" only to be sent a link to a buried Slack thread from three weeks ago?

Relay treats decisions as first-class citizens. Our **decision logs** capture not just the outcome, but the alternatives considered and the people involved. When a developer in Tokyo wakes up, they don't just see that the API spec changed; they understand the reasoning behind it without waking up the lead in New York.

## Video That Respects Your Focus

Sometimes text isn't enough. But nobody watches a 45-minute recording of a meeting they missed.

Relay uses **video snippets**—hard-capped at 2 minutes—that auto-transcribe, auto-summarise, and attach directly to the relevant thread. You can record a quick demo of a bug or a design walk-through, and your team can read the summary or watch the clip at 2x speed.

It’s high-fidelity communication with zero meeting overhead.

## Protecting Your Deep Work

The biggest enemy of distributed teams isn't isolation; it’s interruption.

Relay actively protects your maker time. You can configure **focus windows** where the platform batches notifications and delivers a summary only when you're ready. No more red dots demanding your attention while you're trying to ship code.

We also built **async standups** that actually work. Instead of a daily ritual that interrupts everyone, Relay compiles updates into a team pulse. You see patterns over weeks, not just status updates for today.
