# The Catch-Up Meeting is Dead: Introducing Relay

You know the morning ritual. You grab your coffee, sit down at your desk, and open your laptop. And then the doomscroll begins.

47 unread Slack notifications. Three Loom videos, each over eight minutes long. A Jira ticket that turned into a comment war while you were sleeping.

You spend your first ninety minutes just trying to piece together what happened in San Francisco and Tokyo while you were in London. By the time you’re actually ready to work, you’re already exhausted. And inevitably, someone schedules a "quick sync" to explain what you just spent an hour reading.

This isn't the asynchronous dream we were promised. It’s just synchronous chaos with a time delay.

We built **Relay** because we believe distributed teams deserve better than a choice between notification overload and meeting fatigue.

## The "Async Gap" in Your Stack

Here is the dirty secret of remote work: our tools weren't built for it.

Slack is built for *now*. It demands your immediate attention. If you’re not online when the conversation happens, you’re digging through an archaeological site of context the next morning. It’s noisy, frantic, and anxiety-inducing.

Notion and Confluence are built for *forever*. They are great for static documentation, but they are graveyards for conversation. Decisions get documented there, but the messy, vital context of *how* those decisions were made gets lost in the ether.

So, what fills the gap between the frantic "now" of chat and the static "forever" of docs? Meetings. Endless catch-up meetings.

## Context That Waits for You

Relay is the first collaborative workspace built from the ground up for teams that don’t share a timezone. It’s designed to close the Async Gap.

We don’t just capture messages; we structure narrative.

*   **Timezone-Aware Threads:** Relay understands that your team operates on different clocks. Conversations are structured as narratives, not a chaotic wall of pings. When you log in, Relay surfaces the context relevant to you, organized by priority, not just recency.
*   **Decision Logs:** We treat decisions as first-class citizens. Every major decision captures the alternatives considered, the stakeholders involved, and the reasoning. You don't just see the outcome; you understand the *why*.
*   **Smart Video Snippets:** Video updates are limited to two minutes and auto-transcribed. They attach directly to the relevant thread. You get the nuance of voice and face without the burden of an hour-long recording that nobody watches.
*   **Focus Windows:** The platform actively protects your maker time. Notifications are batched and summarized during your configured deep work blocks.
