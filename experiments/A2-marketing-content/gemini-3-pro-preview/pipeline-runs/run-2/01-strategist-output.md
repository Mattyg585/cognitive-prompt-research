# Creative Brief: Relay Public Launch

## 1. Strategic Foundation
- **Target Audience:** Engineering managers and tech leads at mid-sized companies (50-500 employees) managing distributed teams across 3+ timezones. They are exhausted by "always-on" culture and "timezone tetris."
- **Reader's Goal:** To find a sustainable way to collaborate that doesn't rely on 10 PM meetings or waking up to a wall of chaotic notifications. They want to protect their team's deep work time while maintaining velocity.
- **Core Insight:** True async isn't just "writing it down" — it's about *temporal context*. Current tools treat all communication as urgent; Relay treats communication as a narrative that waits for you.
- **Tone & Voice:** Direct, empathetic, and "smart friend." Confident in the solution but not arrogant. Witty where appropriate, but focused on the real pain of distributed friction.

## 2. Content Structure
- **Working Title:** *Stop Playing Timezone Tetris: Introducing Relay* (Alt: *The End of the 7 AM Standup*)
- **Hook Strategy:** Open with the visceral reality of distributed friction: the 11 PM Slack ping, the 6 AM standup, the feeling of being "always on" but never in sync. Acknowledge that the current toolstack (Slack/Zoom) wasn't built for this.
- **Key Argument 1: The "Async Lie".** Most teams claim to be async but are actually just "slow synchronous." They use chat tools that demand immediate attention or create FOMO. We need a platform built from the ground up for *time-shifted* collaboration, not just text-based chat.
- **Key Argument 2: Context Over Noise.** Introduce Relay's core differentiator: Timezone-aware threads and decision logs. Explain how the platform structures updates as a narrative for when the user comes online, rather than a chaotic stream of unread badges. Mention video snippets that replace hour-long recordings.
- **Key Argument 3: Results, Not Hype.** Validate the approach with beta data. 200 teams, 35% reduction in meetings, 73% DAU. Quote the beta user about the "catch-up just happening." This isn't theoretical; it's working.
- **Call to Action:** Direct the reader to stop the madness and try Relay. Highlight the Free Tier (up to 5 users) as a low-risk way to start protecting their team's focus time.
