# Stop Playing Timezone Tetris: The End of the 7 AM Standup

We’ve all been there. It’s 10:30 PM on a Tuesday. You’ve just closed your laptop, exhaled, and started to unwind. Then your phone buzzes.

It’s the London team. Or maybe Tokyo. A quick question about the API specs. 

Do you ignore it and risk blocking them for eight hours? Or do you reply, get sucked back in, and realize an hour later that your brain is now fully awake and wired?

This is the dirty secret of distributed work. We were promised freedom—work from anywhere, live where you want. But what we actually got was a 24-hour workday broken into fragments of anxiety. We’re playing a never-ending game of "timezone Tetris," trying to fit our lives into the tiny gaps between global meetings and late-night notifications.

The problem isn't the distributed model. The problem is that we’re trying to run 2024 engineering teams with 2014 tools.

We think it’s time to stop.

## The "Async" Lie We Tell Ourselves

Most companies claim to be "async-first." But look at how they actually work, and you’ll see something very different.

They use chat tools designed for immediate gratification. A red dot demands attention *now*. A question buried in a thread creates FOMO if you aren’t reading along in real-time. So, even if you aren't in a meeting, you're tethered to the stream. You’re performing what we call "slow synchronous" work—constantly monitoring, constantly reacting, never truly deep in flow.

When collaboration feels like an interruption, you get two bad outcomes: burnt-out engineers who can't focus, or stalled projects waiting for a timezone overlap that only happens at 6 AM.

We built **Relay** because we were tired of choosing between our sleep and our velocity.

## Context Over Noise: How Relay Works

Relay isn’t just another chat app. It’s a collaborative workspace built specifically for teams where nobody is in the same timezone. Think of it as the child of Notion, Linear, and Loom—but raised by people who understand the difference between *urgent* and *important*.

The core philosophy of Relay is simple: **Context should wait for you, not scream at you.**

Here’s what that looks like in practice:

**Timezone-Aware Threads**  
In Slack, a conversation is a linear stream of chaos. In Relay, threads are narrative-driven. When you log in, Relay knows you’ve been offline. It doesn’t just show you unread badges; it structures the updates into a story of "what happened while you were asleep." You get the context, the decisions, and the blockers, served to you when *you* are ready to work.

**Decision Logs, Not Scroll-Backs**  
Ever try to find out *why* a decision was made by scrolling up 400 messages? We hated that too. Relay introduces **Decision Logs**. Every major choice captures the context, the alternatives considered, and who signed off. Your team in San Francisco can see the decision made in Berlin without needing a forensic investigation of the chat history. They don't just see the outcome; they understand the reasoning.

**Video That Respects Your Time**  
Sometimes text isn't enough, but a meeting is too much. Relay includes **Video Snippets**—capped at 2 minutes. But here’s the magic: they auto-transcribe and auto-summarize. You can watch the update, or just read the summary. No more 45-minute Loom recordings that nobody has time to watch.
