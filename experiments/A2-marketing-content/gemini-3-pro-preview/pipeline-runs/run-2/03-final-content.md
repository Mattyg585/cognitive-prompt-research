# Stop Playing Timezone Tetris: The End of the 7 AM Standup

We’ve all been there. It’s 10:30 PM on a Tuesday. You’ve just closed your laptop, exhaled, and started to unwind. Then your phone buzzes.

It’s the London team. Or maybe Tokyo. A quick question about the API specs.

Do you ignore it and risk blocking them for eight hours? Or do you reply, get sucked back in, and realize an hour later that your brain is fully wired?

This is the dirty secret of distributed work. We were promised freedom—work from anywhere, live where you want. But what we got was a 24-hour workday broken into fragments of anxiety. We’re playing a never-ending game of "timezone Tetris," trying to fit our lives into the tiny gaps between global meetings and late-night notifications.

The problem isn't the distributed model. The problem is running 2024 engineering teams with 2014 tools.

We think it’s time to stop.

## The "Async" Lie We Tell Ourselves

Most companies claim to be "async-first." But look at how they actually work, and you’ll see something very different.

They use chat tools designed for immediate gratification. A red dot demands attention *now*. A question buried in a thread creates FOMO if you aren’t reading along in real-time. Even if you aren't in a meeting, you're tethered to the stream. 

You’re performing "slow synchronous" work—constantly monitoring, constantly reacting, never truly deep in flow.

When collaboration feels like an interruption, you get two bad outcomes: 
*   Burnt-out engineers who can't focus.
*   Stalled projects waiting for a timezone overlap that only happens at 6 AM.

We built **Relay** because we were tired of choosing between our sleep and our velocity.

## Context Over Noise: How Relay Works

Relay isn’t just another chat app. It’s a collaborative workspace built specifically for teams where nobody is in the same timezone. Think of it as the child of Notion, Linear, and Loom—raised by people who understand the difference between *urgent* and *important*.

The core philosophy is simple: **Context should wait for you, not scream at you.**

Here’s what that looks like in practice:

### Timezone-Aware Threads
In Slack, a conversation is a linear stream of chaos. In Relay, threads are narrative-driven. When you log in, Relay knows you’ve been offline. It doesn’t just show unread badges; it structures updates into a story of "what happened while you were asleep." You get context, decisions, and blockers served when *you* are ready.

### Decision Logs, Not Scroll-Backs
Ever try to find out *why* a decision was made by scrolling up 400 messages? We hated that too. Relay introduces **Decision Logs**. Every major choice captures the context, alternatives considered, and sign-offs. Your San Francisco team can see the decision made in Berlin without a forensic investigation. They don't just see the outcome; they understand the reasoning.

### Video That Respects Your Time
Sometimes text isn't enough, but a meeting is too much. Relay includes **Video Snippets**—capped at 2 minutes. The magic? They auto-transcribe and auto-summarize. Watch the update or just read the summary. No more 45-minute recordings that nobody watches.

## Protect Your Deep Work

We aren't here to dunk on Slack or tell you to delete Zoom. Real-time has its place. But it shouldn't be the default setting for your entire company.

Relay is designed to protect your most valuable resource: maker time. With configurable **Deep Work Windows**, the platform shields you from noise, batching notifications so you can code for four hours without a single interruption.

It’s time to stop apologizing for being offline. It’s time to stop scheduling meetings at 7 AM.
