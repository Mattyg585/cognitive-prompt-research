# The End of the Daily "Catch-Up": Introducing Relay for Distributed Teams

You know the feeling. The "distributed morning tax."

You wake up, grab your coffee, and open your laptop. Before you’ve even had a sip, you’re greeted by a wall of bold text in Slack and a dozen missed Zoom links. Your team in London has been working for five hours, and your developers in Sydney are signing off. You’re not just starting your day; you’re already behind.

So what do you do? You schedule the dreaded 8 AM "sync." You drag everyone onto a call—half of them groggy, the other half ready for dinner—just to recite what happened while you were asleep.

It’s exhausting. It’s inefficient. And frankly, it’s a failure of imagination.

## Synchronous Tools Are Failing Global Teams

We’re trying to run global teams with tools built for local offices. Slack and Zoom are optimized for "now." They demand presence. When you use them for everything, you create a culture where being responsive is more important than being productive.

This "always-on" anxiety is why your engineers are burned out. It’s why you feel like a dispatcher instead of a manager.

We built Relay because we believe **context shouldn’t expire the moment it scrolls off the screen.**

## Decouple Context from Presence

The problem isn’t that your team is in different timezones. The problem is that your context is trapped in ephemeral chats.

Relay treats context as a durable asset, not a fleeting stream.

*   **Timezone-aware threads:** Conversations don’t just pile up; they surface the right information when *you* come online.
*   **Decision logs:** Capture the "why" behind every choice, so you can understand the full story without having to be there for the argument.

One of our beta users put it best: *"We stopped having catch-up meetings. The catch-up just... happens."*

## Protect Your Focus, Not Just Your Calendar

Real work requires deep work. Yet most tools are designed to steal your attention, not protect it.

Relay is different. We built features specifically to guard your maker time:

*   **Focus Windows:** Automatically mute interruptions during deep work blocks.
*   **Async Standups:** You shouldn’t have to pause your code to say "I'm working on code."

The results are clear. Teams using Relay have seen a **35% reduction in meetings**. They didn't ban meetings; they just stopped needing them to know what was going on.

Our users agree. With **73% daily active users** (compared to the industry average of 40%), it’s clear that engineers love tools that respect their time.
