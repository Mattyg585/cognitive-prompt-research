Relay is an **async collaboration platform** built for that reality. Not “async‑friendly.” Async‑first. ## The real problem isn’t async—it's context decay
Distributed teams don’t fail because people don’t care. They fail because context disappears between timezones. Decisions get logged as outcomes, not reasoning. Threads become timelines, not narratives. Meetings expand to cover what the tools didn’t.

Most collaboration tools were designed for real‑time proximity: quick pings, live conversations, status updates that expire within hours. But for **distributed teams**, the critical question isn’t “What are we talking about right now?” It’s “What happened while I was offline, and why?”

Relay answers that question by making context the default, not a follow‑up chore.

## Meet Relay: built for timezone collaboration from the ground up
Relay isn’t a mash‑up of docs, chat, and tickets. It’s a system that assumes your team is never online at the same time—and designs every interaction accordingly. Here’s what that looks like in practice.

### Timezone‑aware threads that read like a story
In Relay, a thread isn’t just a chronological list of messages. It’s a narrative that adapts to when you’re reading it. When you open Relay in the morning, you see the story of what happened while you were offline: key updates, decisions, and blockers—organized so you can understand the arc, not just the noise.

This is the feature beta teams loved most. It showed up in 80% of feedback, and it’s why a VP of Engineering told us, “We stopped having catch‑up meetings. The catch‑up just… happens.”

### Decision logs that keep the “why,” not just the “what”
Async works only when people trust the reasoning behind decisions they didn’t witness. Relay captures that reasoning by default. Every decision log includes context, alternatives considered, and who weighed in. That means your team can move fast without leaving anyone behind—and new teammates can get up to speed without digging through old chats.

### Video snippets that respect attention
We all know the pain of the 45‑minute recording nobody watches. Relay replaces that with two‑minute video snippets that auto‑transcribe, auto‑summarize, and attach to the right thread. You get the nuance of voice without the time sink of long playback.

### Async standups that reveal real trends
Daily updates are useful; patterns are better. Relay’s async standups compile into a team pulse, surfacing trends over weeks—what’s blocked, what’s accelerating, and where workload is shifting. It’s visibility without the performative “What did you do yesterday?” theater.

### Deep work protection that’s more than a setting
Most tools offer “Do Not Disturb.” Relay makes focus a first‑class concept. You can set focus windows where notifications are batched and summarized rather than delivered in real time. The platform doesn’t just silence the noise—it organizes it, so you return to a digest, not a distraction spiral.
