# Content Creation Skill (Optimised)

## Purpose
Create channel-ready marketing content by separating **message discovery** (divergent) from **channel execution** (convergent). This prevents SEO and template rules from suppressing creative angles.

## Required Inputs (ask if missing)
- Product/service, audience, channel(s), campaign goal
- Offer/value proposition
- Proof points (metrics, testimonials, differentiators)
- Tone/voice and brand constraints
- Keywords (if SEO is required)
- Compliance or legal constraints

## Phase 1 — Message Discovery (divergent)
**Scope boundary:** explore angles and messaging *before* applying templates or SEO rules.

Use lenses to surface angles:
- What tension, friction, or unmet desire does the audience feel?
- What transformation or outcome do they want?
- What belief can we reframe or challenge?
- What proof points make the claim credible?
- What story or moment makes the problem feel real?
- What action should the reader take next?

**Output: Messaging Brief (structured, concise)**
- Audience & context
- Core tension / job-to-be-done
- Primary promise / transformation
- Proof points (bullets)
- Voice/tone notes
- CTA intent
- Keywords (if provided)
- Angle options (distinct framings; no ranking)

## Phase 2 — Channel Execution (convergent)
**Scope boundary:** apply channel structure and SEO *after* selecting an angle.

Guidance:
- Use only the template for the selected channel(s). Do not blend channels.
- Keep structure flexible; add or remove sections as needed.
- Follow any explicit length/format constraints from the brief; otherwise use channel norms.
- Use headline/hook formulas only as inspiration **after** the angle is chosen.

## Channel Templates (conditional)
### Blog Post
- Headline (benefit-led; include primary keyword if provided)
- Introduction (hook + promise + context)
- Body sections with H2/H3 as needed
- Conclusion + CTA
- Meta description (concise; include primary keyword if provided)
- Optional SEO items if requested: title tag, URL slug, internal/external links, image alt text

### Social Media Post
- Hook line
- Body (short narrative or point-form)
- CTA
- Hashtags if platform expects them (keep relevant)

### Email Newsletter
- Subject line
- Preview text
- Header/hero line
- Scannable body blocks
- One primary CTA
- Footer elements if required (unsubscribe, company info)

### Landing Page
- Headline
- Subheadline
- Hero section with primary CTA
- Value propositions
- Social proof
- Objection handling / FAQ
- Final CTA

### Press Release
- Headline (newsworthy, factual)
- Optional subheadline
- Dateline
- Lead paragraph (who/what/when/where/why)
- Body paragraphs with quotes and context
- Boilerplate
- Media contact

### Case Study
- Title
- Snapshot (customer, industry, product, key outcome)
- Challenge
- Solution
- Results (quantified if available)
- Quote
- CTA

## Headline & Hook Lenses (use after angle selection)
- What outcome matters most to the audience right now?
- What obstacle or tension can we name directly?
- What surprising contrast or contrarian truth is credible here?
- What proof point can we lead with to earn trust?

## SEO Guidance (only when relevant)
- If keywords are provided, use the primary keyword in the headline, early copy, a subheading, and meta description.
- Use secondary keywords naturally.
- Avoid keyword stuffing.
- If an SEO checklist is requested, provide it separately from the draft.

## Quality Check (post-draft)
- Does the copy align with the chosen angle and audience tension?
- Is the tone consistent and brand-appropriate?
- Is there a clear, single primary CTA?
- Does the structure fit the channel and the brief?
- Does the length and detail level vary naturally with input complexity?
