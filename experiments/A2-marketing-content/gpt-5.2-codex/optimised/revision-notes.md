# Revision Notes

## Summary
This revision separates divergent message discovery from convergent formatting/SEO, replaces formula-heavy seeds with exploratory lenses, and relaxes numeric anchors so output varies with input complexity.

## Mapping of architect findings → changes
- **Generation + evaluation in the same context** → Added a two-phase scope boundary: Messaging Brief first, channel execution and SEO second. Quality checks happen post‑draft rather than during ideation.
- **Template saturation across channels** → Templates are conditional on the selected channel(s); instructions explicitly forbid blending channels and prompt to ask when channel is missing.
- **Seeds dominating ideation** → Replaced headline/hook formulas with “Headline & Hook Lenses” and added audience‑tension lenses for angle discovery.
- **Numeric anchors (counts/lengths)** → Removed fixed section counts and length targets; guidance now says “as needed” and to follow explicit constraints only when provided.
- **Fill‑the‑box output structure** → Output formats are flexible; SEO checklist is optional and separated from the main draft.

## What to watch for in testing
- Greater variation in structure/length based on topic complexity.
- Less formulaic hooks and CTAs; more distinct positioning tied to audience tension.
- Clear separation between creative angle selection and format/SEO compliance.
