**Audience & context**
- Engineering managers and tech leads at 50–500 person companies, teams split across 3+ timezones.
- Current stack is a patchwork of Notion/Linear/Slack/Loom/Confluence; async work exists but the coordination tax is high.

**Core tension / job-to-be-done**
- Keep distributed teams aligned without drowning them in meetings, pings, or context loss while people are asleep.

**Primary promise / transformation**
- Relay makes async collaboration feel coherent and human: the catch‑up happens automatically, decisions stay understandable, and deep work is protected.

**Proof points**
- 200 private‑beta teams; 73% daily active usage (vs ~40% category avg).
- NPS 67.
- 35% average meeting reduction reported.
- 80% of feedback mentioned timezone‑aware threads as most‑loved.
- VP Eng quote: “We stopped having catch‑up meetings. The catch‑up just… happens.”

**Voice / tone notes**
- Direct, empathetic to meeting fatigue and notification overload.
- Confident but not combative; lightly witty where it fits.

**CTA intent**
- Invite readers to start a free trial and bring their team’s next sprint into Relay.

**Keywords**
- Primary: async collaboration platform
- Secondary: distributed teams, timezone collaboration, async-first

**Angle options**
- **Angle: The catch‑up that happens while you sleep**
  - Insight: The worst tax of distributed teams is context loss across timezones, not just fewer meetings.
  - Framing: Relay turns overnight updates into a readable narrative so mornings start with clarity, not a scroll.
  - Proof: Timezone‑aware threads; 80% favorite feature; quote about catch‑up meetings disappearing.
- **Angle: Decision‑making that survives timezones**
  - Insight: Teams don’t just need outcomes — they need the why, especially when they weren’t awake for the debate.
  - Framing: Decision logs capture context and alternatives so alignment persists across time.
  - Proof: Decision logs feature; NPS 67; beta adoption across 200 teams.
- **Angle: Async without the notification hangover**
  - Insight: “Async” often means more noise; people need protected focus and batched clarity.
  - Framing: Relay is built async‑first with focus windows and summaries that protect maker time.
  - Proof: Deep work protection + summarised threads; 35% meeting reduction.
- **Angle: Video updates that respect attention**
  - Insight: Distributed updates need human nuance, but long recordings waste time.
  - Framing: Two‑minute snippets with auto‑transcripts and summaries keep updates human and efficient.
  - Proof: 2‑minute max + auto‑summarise feature; high daily usage.
- **Angle: One workspace, fewer tools, less drift**
  - Insight: Teams today stitch together tools not designed for async; drift happens in the seams.
  - Framing: Relay blends docs, decisions, updates, and threads into a single async‑native flow.
  - Proof: “Notion meets Linear meets Loom” positioning; 73% DAU in beta.
