## The problem: tools that assume everyone is awake

Distributed teams don’t fail because they’re remote. They fail because their tools assume a shared clock.

Most products were designed for “everyone is online right now”:

- Threads that make sense only if you read them live
- Notifications that reward fast responders, not thoughtful ones
- Decisions that land as outcomes, not reasoning
- Status updates scattered across standups, DMs, and calendar invites

The result isn’t just annoyance—it’s a steady tax on focus. People context-switch to keep up, or they miss context and lose confidence. Either way, the team ships slower and meets more.

Relay is our attempt to flip that equation: make **timezone collaboration** feel coherent, even when nobody overlaps.

## Meet Relay: an async-first collaboration platform (by design)

Relay isn’t “Notion plus a few templates,” or “Slack but quieter.” It’s the workspace we wanted when our own team started stretching across timezones.

We built Relay for engineering managers and tech leads at companies with **50–500 people**, where:

- Teams span **3+ timezones**
- Work is a mix of product delivery, incident response, and technical decision-making
- The cost of one misunderstood decision shows up weeks later

Relay combines the parts you already use—docs, issues, updates, and video—with one guiding principle:

> **Async should read like a narrative, not a notification feed.**

That principle shows up everywhere, from how threads surface context to how decisions get captured.

## What makes Relay different (and actually useful for timezones)

### 1) Timezone-aware threads that surface context, not noise

Relay’s **timezone-aware threads** automatically surface the right context for whoever is reading, based on when they’re online.

Instead of 38 pings and half a conversation, you get a structured story:

- What changed while you were away
- Why it changed
- What’s waiting on you

It’s the difference between “here are the messages” and “here’s what happened.”

### 2) Decision logs that preserve the *why*, not just the *what*

A decision without context is a future bug.

Relay’s **decision logs** capture:

- The problem being solved
- The options considered (and why some were rejected)
- Who weighed in
- The final call and next steps

So when someone in another timezone asks “why did we do it this way?”, the answer isn’t “scroll up.” It’s already there.

### 3) Video snippets (2 minutes max) with transcripts and summaries

Sometimes a quick explanation is faster than typing—especially when it’s nuanced.

Relay supports **2‑minute video snippets** that automatically:

- Transcribe
- Summarize
- Attach to the relevant thread

No more hour-long recordings. No more “I’ll watch later” lies you tell yourself.

### 4) Async standups that compile into a team pulse

Daily updates are useful—until they become another meeting, another channel, another place to miss information.

Relay’s **async standups** give your team a structured format for updates, then compile them into a living pulse you can scan:

- What’s moving
- What’s stuck
- What patterns are repeating over weeks

It’s visibility without surveillance, and alignment without the calendar.

### 5) Deep work protection that respects maker time

If your tools interrupt you constantly, your “productive day” becomes a series of small panics.

Relay includes configurable **focus windows** where notifications are batched and summarized. You choose the boundaries; Relay enforces them.

Because shipping matters. And so does sanity.
