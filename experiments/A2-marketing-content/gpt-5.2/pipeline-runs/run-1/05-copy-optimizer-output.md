### The problem (recognition)
**You come online and ask:**
- What shipped while I was asleep?
- Why did we change direction?
- What did we decide—and where’s the reasoning?

**Instead, you get:**
- Chat scrollback + scattered docs
- Decisions with missing context

### The promise (outcome)
Relay helps you **wake up to clarity**:
- A narrative of what moved forward (not just what was said)
- Decision context that’s easy to verify
- A clear “what needs you next” view—without dragging everyone into a meeting

### How it works
**1) Durable threads (context stays attached to the work)**
Keep discussions in structured threads so the “why” doesn’t disappear into drive-by pings.

**2) Decision context that travels**
Capture the reasoning as you go. Relay keeps decisions, links, and supporting context nearby so you can validate fast.

**3) What-needs-you-next (signal over noise)**
Come online to a prioritized view of what changed, what’s blocked, and where you’re needed next.

### Objections / FAQ
**Is this just another chat tool or an ‘AI summary’ layer?**
No. Relay is async-first: durable threads and decision context are the core. Summaries can help you onboard quickly, but the underlying context stays navigable and verifiable.

**Do we have to replace Slack/Linear/Notion overnight?**
No. Start with one project or team where context loss is painful. Expand only if it fits your workflow.

**Are auto-summaries accurate enough to trust?**
Treat summaries as a fast on-ramp—not a source of truth. Relay keeps thread history, decisions, and links so you can verify when it matters.

**Does this turn into status theater?**
Relay is built for shared awareness, not surveillance: what changed, what’s blocked, what needs attention—without requiring everyone to be online at the same time.
