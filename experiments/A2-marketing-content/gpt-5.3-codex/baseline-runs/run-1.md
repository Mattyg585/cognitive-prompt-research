# Relay: The Async Collaboration Platform Built for Distributed Teams

If your team works across three or more timezones, you already know the pattern. Your mornings begin with a catch-up ritual: scanning Slack threads, replaying Loom videos, checking project docs, and trying to reconstruct decisions from fragments. By the time you understand what happened, half your focus window is gone.

That workflow is not a team problem. It is a tooling problem.

Today, Threadline is publicly launching **Relay**, an **async collaboration platform** designed for engineering teams that do not share a clock. After eight months in private beta with 200 teams, Relay has shown what happens when collaboration software is built async-first from day one: less meeting drag, better decision continuity, and more protected deep work.

## The problem with "mostly async" tools

Most tools distributed teams use today were created for real-time offices and later adapted for async workflows. The result is familiar:

- Notifications become a timeline, not a narrative.
- Decisions are recorded as outcomes, not context.
- Video updates are too long to watch and too hard to search.
- Daily updates disappear into channels without long-term visibility.

These tools are powerful in isolation, but they do not reduce the cost of context transfer across timezones. For engineering managers and tech leads, that cost appears as repeated clarification, duplicate decisions, and more "quick sync" meetings than anyone planned.

## What Relay changes for distributed teams

Relay combines documentation, execution tracking, and lightweight video into one workflow that assumes teammates are often offline.

### Timezone-aware threads

Instead of dropping everyone into a wall of notifications, Relay surfaces what happened while you were asleep as a coherent narrative. You see the state of the conversation, decisions made, and unresolved points in sequence.

### Decision logs with rationale

Every decision in Relay includes context, alternatives considered, and who participated. Team members in other timezones can understand not only what changed, but why it changed.

### Video snippets with transcripts

Relay limits update videos to two minutes, automatically transcribes them, generates summaries, and links them to the relevant discussion thread. Teams keep the speed of video without accumulating one-hour recordings nobody revisits.

### Async standups with trend visibility

Daily check-ins roll up into a team pulse that can be reviewed over time. Managers can identify recurring blockers and momentum patterns instead of managing one day at a time.

### Deep work protection

Relay supports configurable focus windows that batch and summarize notifications. Engineers can stay available while still protecting maker time.
