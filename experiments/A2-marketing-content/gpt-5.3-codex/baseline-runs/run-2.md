# Relay: An Async Collaboration Platform for Teams Across Timezones

Distributed engineering teams are not short on tools. They are short on continuity.

Most teams can point to the same symptoms: unread message backlogs, repeated status clarification, and meetings created mostly to recover context that already existed somewhere. The real issue is not communication effort; it is communication architecture.

Today, Threadline is launching Relay publicly after an eight-month private beta with 200 teams. Relay is an **async collaboration platform** built specifically for teams that collaborate across multiple timezones and need reliable handoffs without constant live overlap.

## Why existing collaboration stacks still create catch-up work

Current stacks often combine chat, docs, issue tracking, and video tools. Each tool is useful, but the combination creates friction:

1. Context is spread across systems.
2. Decisions are visible, but rationale is often missing.
3. Video updates are fast to record but expensive to consume.
4. Notification streams prioritize immediacy over coherence.

For engineering managers, this leads to predictable costs: duplicated decisions, slower onboarding into active threads, and recurring "sync" meetings that act as manual context rebuilds.

## Core capabilities in Relay

Relay was designed around asynchronous continuity from the start.

### 1. Timezone-aware threads

Relay organizes thread updates so each reader can quickly understand what happened while offline. Instead of raw notification streams, the platform emphasizes sequence and context.

### 2. Decision logs that keep reasoning attached

Each decision captures:
- the decision itself,
- alternatives considered,
- participating stakeholders,
- surrounding context.

This makes downstream execution easier for contributors who were not online during the original discussion.

### 3. Two-minute video snippets with automation

Relay encourages concise video updates and automatically:
- transcribes content,
- summarizes key points,
- attaches snippets to relevant threads.

Teams keep expressive communication without accumulating long videos that are difficult to process.

### 4. Async standups that show longitudinal patterns

Daily updates compile into a team-level pulse view. Leaders can inspect trends over weeks rather than reacting only to daily snapshots.

### 5. Deep work protection via focus windows

Relay batches and summarizes notifications during configured focus windows, helping contributors preserve uninterrupted maker time.

## Intended users

Relay targets engineering managers and technical leads in organizations of roughly 50 to 500 employees with teams distributed across at least three timezones.

These teams usually have strong execution cadence and clear product pressure. They need better collaboration mechanics without adding process overhead.
