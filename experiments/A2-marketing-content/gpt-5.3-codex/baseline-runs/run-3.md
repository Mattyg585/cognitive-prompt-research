# Relay: The Async Collaboration Platform Built for Timezone Collaboration

Engineering teams that work across timezones do not need more notifications. They need more usable context.

That distinction is why Threadline built Relay, now launching publicly after an eight-month private beta with 200 teams. Relay is an **async collaboration platform** designed for distributed teams that want fewer meetings, clearer decisions, and stronger focus time.

## The collaboration gap distributed teams still face

Even high-performing teams frequently rely on catch-up rituals:

- scanning multiple channels for status,
- replaying video updates,
- asking for decision background,
- scheduling additional sync meetings.

The issue is rarely commitment or talent. It is that traditional tools assume shared working hours, while distributed teams operate in staggered windows.

## How Relay approaches async-first collaboration

Relay combines thread context, decision transparency, concise video communication, and status rhythm into one system built for timezone handoff.

### Timezone-aware threads provide narrative continuity

When teammates log in from different regions, they need more than chronological message history. Relay surfaces contextual thread summaries so people can understand progression and next steps quickly.

### Decision logs preserve the "why"

Decisions include alternatives, reasoning, and participants. Team members who were offline can recover intent without waiting for a live debrief.

### Video snippets are short, searchable, and attached to context

Relay constrains updates to two-minute snippets and adds transcription plus automatic summarization. This reduces the consumption cost of asynchronous video communication.

### Async standups become a team pulse

Daily updates roll into longitudinal signals. Managers can identify trends, recurring blockers, and execution drift over time.

### Focus windows protect maker time

Notifications are batched and summarized during configured deep-work windows. Teams remain informed while reducing interruption frequency.

## Positioning versus existing tools

Teams often combine Notion, Linear, Slack, Loom, and Confluence. Relay does not replace the idea of collaboration software; it rethinks the default posture. Instead of treating async as an add-on behavior, Relay treats async as the baseline condition.

That design choice affects every product surface, from thread rendering to decision capture to notification flow.
