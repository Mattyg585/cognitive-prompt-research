# Your Catch-Up Meeting Is a Symptom. Relay Fixes the Cause.

Every distributed engineering manager has a version of the same morning.

You open Slack to 90+ unread messages. Someone dropped a Loom at 1:14 a.m. with "quick context." A decision was made in a thread you were not awake for. Another teammate asks, "Can someone summarize where we landed?"

By 9:40, you have done a lot of communication and almost no engineering leadership.

The catch-up meeting that gets added to the calendar is not the problem. It is the symptom.

Today Threadline is launching Relay publicly — an **async collaboration platform** built for teams that do not share a timezone, and do not want to run their week inside recovery meetings.

## The issue isn't effort. It's architecture.

Most distributed teams are trying to do async work on tools designed for synchronous presence. Slack, Notion, Loom, Linear, Confluence — all useful. But stitched together, they force humans to do manual context transfer.

That is the hidden tax:

- Reconstructing intent from partial threads
- Repeating decisions for teammates who were offline
- Turning "just read the updates" into a 45-minute ritual
- Trading deep work for notification hygiene

Relay was built around one assumption: teammates will often be offline when work happens.

## What Relay changes in practice

### 1) Timezone-aware threads that read like a narrative

Instead of a raw pile of notifications, Relay surfaces what happened while you slept as a coherent sequence: what changed, why it changed, and what still needs input.

### 2) Decision logs with reasoning attached

Most tools capture the outcome. Relay captures the decision context too — alternatives considered, participants involved, and rationale. That means a teammate in another timezone can move forward without a live debrief.

### 3) Two-minute video snippets that are actually usable

Relay keeps video updates short, transcribes them automatically, summarizes the key points, and links them to the relevant thread. You keep the nuance of voice without asking people to watch one-hour recordings.

### 4) Async standups that show patterns over time

Daily updates compile into a team pulse, so you can see trends, not just snapshots. You notice recurring blockers before they become delivery risk.

### 5) Focus windows that protect maker time

Relay batches and summarizes notifications during configured focus windows. Your team stays informed without living in interruption mode.
