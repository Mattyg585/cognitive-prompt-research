# Distributed Teams Don’t Need More Messages. They Need Better Memory.

The pain of distributed work is usually described as a communication problem.

It is not.

Your team is already communicating all day. The real problem is memory: what decisions were made, what context mattered, what changed, and what should happen next when someone in another timezone logs in six hours later.

That is where most tools quietly fail. They store activity, not continuity.

Today, Threadline is publicly launching Relay, an **async collaboration platform** built for distributed teams that want fewer meetings and better handoffs.

## Async-first is not a feature. It is a design posture.

Most collaboration stacks were built for real-time offices and retrofitted for asynchronous teams. You can feel that mismatch in the daily workflow:

- updates are fragmented,
- decisions are detached from rationale,
- video context is expensive to consume,
- notifications hijack focus.

Relay starts from the opposite assumption: people will be offline when important things happen. The product is designed so work remains legible when that happens.

## What that looks like inside Relay

### Timezone-aware threads

Relay turns "what happened while I was asleep" into a readable narrative. You can enter a thread and understand state quickly without reverse-engineering message history.

### Decision logs with context

Each decision records alternatives and reasoning, not just outcomes. That single change removes a surprising amount of repeated discussion.

### Two-minute video snippets + automatic transcript and summary

You get the speed and nuance of video, but in a format that is searchable, skimmable, and connected to the exact thread where it matters.

### Async standups with trend visibility

Standups become a longitudinal signal. You can see when quality of updates drops, when blockers repeat, and where delivery friction accumulates.

### Deep work protection

Focus windows batch notifications into digestible summaries so engineers can stay responsive without living in constant context-switch.
