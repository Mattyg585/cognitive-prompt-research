If you lead a distributed engineering team, you've probably had this thought at least once:

"We believe in async. Why does it still feel chaotic?"

The honest answer is uncomfortable. Most teams are trying to run async-first workflows on products designed for synchronous, same-timezone work. Those products are good at real-time collaboration and mediocre at preserving context across handoffs.

Threadline built Relay to solve exactly that. Starting today, Relay is publicly available as an **async collaboration platform** built for distributed teams by default.

## The daily failure mode everyone normalizes

A lot of teams treat these as normal:

- Morning backlog triage before "real work" starts
- Repeated decision explanations for different timezone cohorts
- "Quick" catch-up meetings that consume focus windows
- Long video updates that are rarely fully watched

But normalization does not make the workflow healthy.

Relay is designed to make these failure modes unnecessary.

## Five product behaviors that shift the workflow

### Timezone-aware thread narratives
You get a structured "while you were offline" view instead of fragmented alert history.

### Decision logs with rationale
Outcomes include context and alternatives, so teammates can execute without waiting for synchronous clarification.

### Short video snippets with transcript + summary
Video becomes lightweight context, not asynchronous homework.

### Async standups that aggregate into pulse
Daily updates become useful over time, not disposable status fragments.

### Focus windows for deep work
Notifications are batched and summarized, reducing interruption tax during maker time.
