### PRODUCT INSIGHT
Relay turns cross-timezone collaboration from "notification consumption" into "context continuity." The product value is not another communication channel; it is reduced context-reconstruction labor.

### AUDIENCE MODEL
Primary reader: engineering manager or tech lead in a 50-500 person company with teams across 3+ timezones.

Current pain state:
- starts day in backlog triage instead of delivery leadership
- repeated decision re-explanations for different timezone cohorts
- recurring catch-up meetings disguised as alignment

Likely skepticism:
- "We already have Slack + docs + issue tracker. Why another tool?"
- "Will this create more process overhead?"

Attention trigger: concrete reduction in meeting and context debt, not generic productivity claims.

### BREAKTHROUGH PROOF
- 73% DAU vs ~40% category benchmark -> indicates habitual use, not novelty sampling.
- NPS 67 -> strong product advocacy signal.
- 35% average meeting reduction -> direct workflow impact tied to audience pain.
- 80% mention timezone-aware threads -> differentiator is clear and repeatable.
- Quote: "We stopped having catch-up meetings. The catch-up just... happens." -> compresses whole value proposition into one line.

### NARRATIVE TERRITORY
1. **Catch-up meeting is a symptom, not the disease**
   - Reframes current ritual as architecture failure, not management discipline issue.
2. **Distributed teams have a memory problem**
   - Positions Relay as organizational memory layer for async execution.
3. **Deep work protection as a leadership requirement**
   - Starts from engineering velocity and cognitive load, then introduces features as mechanisms.
