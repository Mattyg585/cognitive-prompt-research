### CHOSEN ANGLE
Catch-up meetings are a visible symptom of a deeper systems problem: collaboration tools preserve activity but not context continuity. Relay removes the symptom by fixing the underlying architecture.

### READER JOURNEY
Start: "My team is disciplined, but async still feels expensive."
Mid-shift: "This is not a people problem; it is a tool-memory problem."
End: "I can test this now with low risk and measurable upside."

### STRUCTURE PLAN
1. **Open with lived morning scenario**
   - Make reader feel recognized quickly.
2. **Name the root cause**
   - Distinguish communication volume from context continuity.
3. **Introduce Relay thesis**
   - Async-first by design, not retrofitted.
4. **Feature-to-workflow mapping**
   - One short subsection per key capability tied to pain relief.
5. **Proof section**
   - Interpret metrics; do not just list them.
6. **Adoption path**
   - Audience fit + pricing + low-friction trial logic.
7. **Close with momentum**
   - CTA as practical test, not hard sell.

### VOICE BRIEF
Do:
- Write like a senior operator explaining a hard-won insight.
- Use short, punchy transitions between sections.
- Keep empathy visible.

Avoid:
- buzzword stacking
- formulaic "In today's fast-paced world"
- over-claiming competitor replacement

### MUST-LAND PROOF
- 73% DAU
- NPS 67
- 35% meeting reduction
- 80% timezone-thread mention
- VP Eng quote exactly once for impact

### HEADLINE INTENT
Create a clear reframe (symptom vs cause) and signal practical value for engineering leaders.

### CTA INTENT
Reader should feel: "This is measurable and low risk. I should pilot this now."
