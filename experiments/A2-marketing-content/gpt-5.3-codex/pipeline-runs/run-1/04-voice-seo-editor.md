# Your Catch-Up Meeting Is a Symptom, Not a Workflow

You know the ritual.

Coffee in hand, Slack open, dozens of unread messages, one Loom labeled "quick update," and at least one decision thread that ended while you were asleep. By the time you understand what happened, your first focus block is gone.

Most distributed engineering teams treat this as normal. It isn't.

The catch-up meeting on your calendar is not the problem. It's the symptom.

Threadline built Relay for exactly this gap, and today it's launching publicly: an **async collaboration platform** for teams that don't share office hours and don't want to spend leadership time reconstructing context.

## The root issue: activity is not continuity

Your current stack probably works. Slack, Notion, Linear, Loom, Confluence. The issue is not that these tools are bad. The issue is that, together, they preserve *activity* better than they preserve *meaning*.

Distributed teams pay for that mismatch every day:

- repeated explanations of decisions to different timezone cohorts,
- recurring meetings that exist mainly to rebuild shared context,
- updates that are technically available but expensive to consume,
- deep work fragmented by notification maintenance.

You don't have a communication deficit. You have a continuity deficit.

## How this async collaboration platform fixes timezone collaboration

### Timezone-aware threads

Instead of throwing you into raw chronology, Relay shows what changed, why it changed, and what still needs input. You get a narrative handoff, not a notification archaeology project.

### Decision logs with rationale

Most teams remember outcomes. Relay records outcomes *and* alternatives considered, context, and participants. New readers can execute faster because they inherit reasoning, not just conclusions.

### Two-minute video snippets with transcript and summary

Video keeps nuance. Length kills adoption. Relay constrains updates to short clips, then transcribes and summarizes automatically so people can scan, search, and move.

### Async standups that reveal trend lines

Daily updates roll into a team pulse. Instead of asking "what happened today?" every day, you can finally ask, "what pattern is forming?"

### Focus windows that protect maker time

Relay batches and summarizes notifications so your team can stay responsive without living in interruption mode.
