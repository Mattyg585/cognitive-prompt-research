# Your Team Doesn’t Need More Messages. It Needs Better Memory.

Distributed engineering teams are talking constantly.

And still, the same sentence gets typed every day: "Can someone summarize what we decided?"

That contradiction is the real problem.

Most teams assume async friction is a communication-volume issue. In practice, it's usually a memory-quality issue. Information exists, but context doesn't survive timezone handoffs cleanly enough to drive execution.

Threadline built Relay for this exact gap. After eight months of private beta, Relay is now publicly launching as an async-first collaboration workspace for teams spread across time.

## Why existing stacks feel noisy but fragile

You probably already have strong tools: chat, docs, issue tracking, maybe video updates.

The weakness appears at boundaries:

- outcomes are documented but rationale is scattered,
- updates are posted but expensive to consume,
- handoffs happen, but confidence decays with each timezone transition.

So teams compensate with meetings. Not because they love meetings, but because meetings are currently the most reliable memory reconstruction mechanism.

## Relay is built as memory architecture

Relay's feature set makes more sense when viewed through one question: "Will a teammate who was offline still understand enough to act?"

### Timezone-aware threads

Relay presents thread history as a coherent narrative handoff rather than a flat notification stream. You get chronology plus meaning.

### Decision logs with alternatives and context

Critical decisions include not just what was chosen, but what else was considered and who participated. This preserves intent, not just recordkeeping.

### Two-minute video snippets with transcript + summary

Video remains useful because it is short and auto-processed. Teammates can scan the summary, read the transcript, or watch the clip depending on need.

### Async standups as team pulse

Standups become cumulative insight instead of disposable updates. Managers can spot repeated blockers and trend shifts earlier.

### Focus windows for deep work

Notification batching protects maker time while preserving visibility through summaries. Teams spend less time in reactive mode.
