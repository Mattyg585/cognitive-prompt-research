## The Catch-Up Problem Nobody Solved

Here's what most collaboration tools get wrong about distributed work: they treat timezone differences as a notification-delivery problem. Messages arrive while you sleep. You wake up and read them. Done, right?

Not even close.

Reading a wall of messages tells you *what* happened. It doesn't tell you what matters, what decisions were made, what the reasoning was, or what you need to act on. So you do the only rational thing — you schedule a meeting to have someone walk you through it.

That "Quick Sync" meeting? It exists because your tools failed to carry context across timezones. Every recurring catch-up meeting on your calendar is a symptom of the same underlying failure.

Our beta teams reduced their meetings by an average of 35%. Not because we told them to have fewer meetings. Because the reason for those meetings disappeared.

## How Relay Actually Works Across Timezones

Three things make Relay different from cobbling together Slack, Notion, Loom, and Linear — and they're all rooted in the same design principle: context should travel with the work, structured for whoever picks it up next.

### Timezone-aware threads

This is the feature 80% of our beta users mentioned unprompted when asked what they love most.

When you open a Relay thread, you don't see a raw chronological feed. You see what happened while you were away, organized as a narrative. Key decisions are highlighted. New information is distinguished from discussion. The thread knows *when* you last saw it and structures the update accordingly.

The difference is subtle until you experience it. Then it's the kind of thing you can't go back from.

### Decision logs that carry the "why"

Every decision in Relay automatically captures the context: what alternatives were considered, what evidence was discussed, who weighed in. When your colleague in Berlin makes a call while you're asleep in Portland, you don't just see the outcome — you understand the reasoning.

This changes something important. You stop second-guessing decisions made in other timezones. You stop reopening settled questions. You start trusting the process, because the process is finally visible.

### Video snippets that respect your time

Two-minute maximum. Auto-transcribed. Auto-summarised. Attached to the relevant thread, not floating in a separate tool.

Think of them as async standup contributions that are actually watchable. No more 45-minute Loom recordings that nobody finishes. The constraint is the feature — it forces clarity.

## Deep Work Isn't a Perk. It's the Point.

Most collaboration platforms measure success by engagement. More messages, more reactions, more time in the tool. Relay measures success differently.

Relay has configurable focus windows — blocks of time where notifications are batched, summarised, and held until you're ready. The platform actively protects your makers' time instead of interrupting it.

This isn't a "do not disturb" toggle. It's a system-level commitment to the idea that async-first means trusting people to engage on their own schedule. Your best engineers shouldn't have to defend their focus time. The tool should defend it for them.
