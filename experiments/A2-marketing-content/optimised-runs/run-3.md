You know the ritual. You open your laptop, and the first thirty minutes disappear into reconstructing what happened while you slept. Scroll through Slack. Skim the Notion doc someone updated at 2 AM your time. Watch the first three minutes of a forty-minute Loom because nobody timestamped the part that matters. Piece it all together. Then join a meeting whose only purpose is to make sure everyone else pieced it together too.

You've accepted this as the cost of working across timezones. It isn't. It's the cost of using tools that were never built for it.

## The tools lied to you (a little)

Every collaboration platform claims to support distributed teams. And they do — the way a sedan supports off-roading. You can make it work. You'll just spend a lot of energy compensating for a vehicle that wasn't designed for the terrain.

Slack was built for real-time conversation. Notion was built for structured documentation. Linear was built for project tracking. Loom was built for one-way video. Each one solves a genuine problem. None of them solves the problem underneath all the others: when your team spans timezones, the context everyone needs is scattered across four tools, and reassembling it is now someone's job.

Usually yours.

## What we built, and why

Relay is an async collaboration platform designed from the ground up for teams where nobody shares a clock. We didn't start with a real-time tool and bolt on async features. We started with a question: what would collaboration look like if the tool understood that your teammates are asleep right now, and that's fine?

It looks like this.

### Timezone-aware threads

When you open Relay in the morning, you don't see a wall of notifications. You see a structured narrative of what happened while you were offline — organized by thread, surfacing the context that matters for where each conversation stands now. Your teammates in Berlin moved a decision forward. Your designer in Auckland left a video snippet. The thread shows you the story, not the raw feed.

This was the feature 80% of our beta users mentioned unprompted in feedback. It's the thing that makes the morning catch-up ritual disappear.

### Decision logs that include the reasoning

In most tools, decisions appear as outcomes. Someone writes "we're going with option B" in a channel, and anyone who wasn't online for the discussion just has to trust it. Relay captures the context: what alternatives were considered, what the tradeoffs were, who weighed in. So when your colleague in São Paulo logs on four hours later, they don't just see what was decided — they understand why. That kills an entire category of "wait, why did we do this?" meetings.

### Video snippets that respect your time

Two minutes, max. Auto-transcribed, auto-summarized, attached to the thread they belong to. No more hour-long recordings that five people need to watch. Record what matters, in context, and move on.

### Deep work protection, built in

Relay doesn't just tolerate focus time — it actively protects it. Configurable focus windows batch and summarize notifications so your makers can actually make things. The platform treats uninterrupted time as a feature, not an inconvenience.
