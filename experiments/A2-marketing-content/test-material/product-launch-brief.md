# Test Material: Product Launch Brief

## The Product

**Relay** — a collaborative workspace platform for distributed engineering teams. Think Notion meets Linear meets Loom, built specifically for teams where nobody is in the same timezone.

### Key Details
- **Company**: Threadline (Series A, 40 employees, founded 2024)
- **Product**: Relay — async-first collaboration platform
- **Launch**: Public launch after 8 months of private beta with 200 teams
- **Pricing**: Free tier (up to 5 users), Pro ($12/user/month), Enterprise (custom)
- **Target audience**: Engineering managers and tech leads at companies with 50-500 employees, distributed across 3+ timezones
- **Competitors**: Notion, Linear, Slack, Loom, Confluence — but none built async-first from the ground up

### What Makes It Different
- **Timezone-aware threads**: Conversations automatically surface context for whoever is reading, based on when they're online. You see what happened while you were asleep, structured as a narrative, not a wall of notifications.
- **Decision logs**: Every decision captures the context, the alternatives considered, and who was involved — so people in other timezones don't just see the outcome, they understand the reasoning.
- **Video snippets with transcripts**: 2-minute max video updates that auto-transcribe, auto-summarise, and attach to the relevant thread. No more hour-long recordings nobody watches.
- **Async standups**: Structured daily updates that compile into a team pulse — visible patterns over weeks, not just today's status.
- **Deep work protection**: Configurable "focus windows" where notifications are batched and summarised. The platform actively protects maker time.

### Beta Results
- 200 teams in private beta
- 73% daily active usage (vs industry avg ~40% for collaboration tools)
- NPS of 67
- Average meeting reduction of 35% reported by beta teams
- Most-loved feature: timezone-aware threads (mentioned in 80% of feedback)
- Quote from beta user (VP Eng, 120-person company): "We stopped having catch-up meetings. The catch-up just... happens."

### Brand Voice
- Direct, not corporate. Write like you're explaining to a smart friend, not presenting to a board.
- Confident but not arrogant. We built something good and we know it, but we're not dunking on Slack.
- Empathetic to the pain. Distributed teams are exhausted by notification overload and meeting fatigue. We get it.
- Slightly witty when it fits. Don't force it.
