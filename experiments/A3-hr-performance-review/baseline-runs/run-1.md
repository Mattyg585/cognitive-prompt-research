### Areas for Development
- **Navigating ambiguity** — Jordan excels when given a well-defined technical problem but stalls when the problem space is fuzzy. When asked to explore open-ended challenges like "figure out our observability strategy," he tends to wait for someone else to define the approach rather than driving the framing himself. To progress toward IC5, Jordan needs to demonstrate he can take an ambiguous problem, break it down, propose an approach, and drive alignment — not just execute once the path is clear.
- **Executive communication** — Updates to the VP of Engineering on the Kafka migration were overly technical, focusing on WAL segments and consumer group rebalancing when the audience needed "is it on track and what are the risks." This required coaching on two separate occasions. Jordan should practice framing updates for non-technical stakeholders by leading with impact, status, and risks before diving into technical details.
- **Capacity management and escalation** — Jordan occasionally takes on too much without flagging when he's stretched. During October, he was carrying both the Kafka migration and the cost optimisation initiative and was clearly overloaded but didn't raise it until it surfaced in a 1:1. Jordan should proactively communicate capacity constraints rather than absorbing pressure silently.
