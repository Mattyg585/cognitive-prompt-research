# Blind Evaluation: Experiment A4 (Design Research Synthesis)

**Model:** Gemini 3 Pro Preview
**Date:** 2026-03-15

## 1. Summary of Outputs

*   **Baseline (Run 1):** A standard, competent research report. Structures findings into Themes, Insights -> Opportunities, and Recommendations. Identifies the core issues ("4 clicks", "Admin vs Maker").
*   **Optimised (Run 1):** A sharper, more strategic report. Uses evocative language ("Update Tax", "Destination vs Habitat") to group findings. Adds a "Strategic Analysis" section.
*   **Pipeline (Runs 1-3):** A "Research Plan" or "Strategic Recommendations" document. It skips the detailed reporting of evidence and jumps straight to synthesis and strategy. It reframes the problem systemically ("Data Trust Spiral", "Compliance-Value failure loop").

## 2. Dimension Scoring

### Depth
*   **Baseline: 3/5.** Covers the expected ground. Identifies the friction points and the user personas. Solid but literal.
*   **Optimised: 4/5.** Goes deeper into *why* the friction matters (habit change vs augmentation). The "Destination vs Habitat" insight is a level up from "Workflow Isolation".
*   **Pipeline: 5/5.** Excellent strategic depth. It identifies the feedback loops causing the failure (e.g., "stale data renders reporting useless -> managers nag -> makers resist"). It reframes the competition not as other tools, but as "text files" and "Slack".

### Specificity
*   **Baseline: 3/5.** References specific data points ("4 clicks", "P1").
*   **Optimised: 4/5.** More specific in its recommendations ("One-Tap", "Headless").
*   **Pipeline: 4/5.** High specificity in solution concepts ("Confidence Score", "Command Line interface"). Less specific on raw user quotes (likely due to its format as a "Plan" rather than "Report").

### Natural Variation (Pipeline only)
*   **Pipeline: 4/5.** The three pipeline runs produced distinct strategic angles. Run 1 focused on "Data Trust Spiral", Run 2 on "Manager-Maker Disconnect", Run 3 on "Compliance-Value failure loop". The "Opportunity Areas" differed significantly (e.g., "Invisible Admin" vs "No-UI Workflow"), showing the model is exploring the solution space rather than regurgitating a template.

### Completeness
*   **Baseline: 4/5.** A complete, standard research report.
*   **Optimised: 4/5.** Also complete, with better distinct sections.
*   **Pipeline: 4/5.** While it condenses the "Evidence" section, it is complete as a strategic document. It integrates the themes into the rationale rather than listing them separately, which is an efficient choice for executive reading.

### Audience Awareness
*   **Baseline: 3/5.** Professional, standard tone.
*   **Optimised: 4/5.** Uses "sticky" language ("Update Tax") that helps stakeholders remember the issues.
*   **Pipeline: 5/5.** Highly tailored for decision-makers. The "Strategic Priorities" table with Impact/Effort is exactly what a PM needs. The "How Might We" framing is perfect for a design team hand-off.

### Domain Dimension: Strategic Reframing (Cataloguing vs Reframing)
*   **Baseline: 2/5 (Cataloguing).** Lists what users said. "Users find 4 clicks too slow."
*   **Optimised: 4/5 (Reframing).** Groups findings into concepts. "The Update Tax."
*   **Pipeline: 5/5 (Reframing).** Fundamentally reframes the product challenge. "The competition is... zero-friction 'shadow' workflows." "Stop building for the Manager... start removing barriers for the Maker."

## 3. Comparison Summary

| Dimension | Baseline | Optimised | Pipeline | Delta (Pipe vs Base) |
|-----------|----------|-----------|----------|----------------------|
| Depth | 3 | 4 | 5 | +2 |
| Specificity | 3 | 4 | 4 | +1 |
| Completeness | 4 | 4 | 4 | 0 |
| Audience Awareness | 3 | 4 | 5 | +2 |
| Strategic Reframing | 2 | 4 | 5 | +3 |

**Overall Preference:** **Pipeline**

**Key Differences:**
1.  **Shift from "What" to "So What":** Baseline reports *what* happened. Optimised explains *why* it matters. Pipeline explains *what we must do* about it strategically.
2.  **Systemic Thinking:** Pipeline identifies the *loops* (Data Trust Spiral) rather than just the *points* (High Friction).
3.  **Actionability:** Pipeline's output is ready for a roadmap discussion. Baseline's output is a document that needs to be interpreted to create a roadmap.

**Magnitude of Difference:** **Large.**
The Pipeline output transforms the raw research into a strategic manifesto. It changes the conversation from "fixing usability bugs" to "pivoting the product strategy". This is a substantial qualitative leap.

## 4. Diagnostic Observations
*   **Format Shift:** The Pipeline output is explicitly a "Research Plan" / "Strategic Recommendations" doc, whereas Baseline/Optimised are "Synthesis Reports". This format shift (likely prompted by the pipeline steps) forces a higher level of synthesis.
*   **Sticky Language:** Both Optimised and Pipeline use strong conceptual labels ("The Anti-Ferrari", "The Invisible Admin"). Baseline is more dry.
*   **Efficiency:** Pipeline packs the most value per word. It condenses the themes into the table rationale, avoiding repetition.
