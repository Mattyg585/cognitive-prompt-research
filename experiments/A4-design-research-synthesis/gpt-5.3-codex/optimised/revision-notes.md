# Revision Notes: A4 Design Research Synthesis (GPT-5.3-Codex)

## Finding → change mapping

### 1) Premature convergence from rigid scaffold (High)
- **Change**: Reframed output as a guide, not a fixed template.
- **Why**: Prevents slot-filling behavior and allows evidence-responsive structure.

### 2) Investigation mixed with evaluation (High)
- **Change**: Removed required impact/effort grid behavior.
- **Why**: Evaluation no longer pre-filters what gets surfaced during investigation.

### 3) Summary-first anchoring (Moderate)
- **Change**: Summary moved to final section with explicit “write last” instruction.
- **Why**: Reduces narrative lock-in before evidence synthesis.

### 4) Hidden count anchors (Moderate)
- **Change**: Removed implied fixed counts for themes, quotes, segments, recommendations.
- **Why**: Restores natural variation based on input complexity.

### 5) Forced insight-opportunity linearity (Moderate)
- **Change**: Replaced rigid table behavior with narrative relationship guidance.
- **Why**: Supports many-to-one and one-to-many reasoning.

### 6) Over-forced segmentation (Moderate)
- **Change**: Segment section now conditional.
- **Why**: Avoids invented segment taxonomies when data does not support them.

## Preserved from original

- Core purpose and accepted inputs.
- Evidence-centric expectation (quotes and participant grounding).
- Recommendations and further-research sections.

## Expected behavioral shift

- Less catalogue output.
- Better weighting by evidence strength.
- More strategic reframing in insight and recommendation logic.
